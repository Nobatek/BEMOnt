Authors:
Nathalie Charbel
Contributors:
Pierre Bourreau

Abstract

The refactored ontology mainly relies on well known existing ontologies (BOT, SAREF4BLDG, SSN, SOSA, BuildingElements, sml-owl and QUDT). IfcOWL is excluded but links to its concepts are maintained in the form of rdfs:seeAlso. The idea behind the refactored model is to be able to import only required modules to answer a particular use case. The modules are summarized as below:
  • Building topology module for modeling building topology and a wide set of building types (including residential and non residential buildings), spaces and storeys based on the Omniclass standard.
  • Sensor module for modeling sensors, observations, features of intereset, and extented sensors and observations properties.
  • Property module for describing physical and occupant properties.
  • Building appliance module for modeling building appliances and specific types.
  • Building Element module for modeling builing elements taxonomy based on IfcBuildingElement subtree in the IFC specification.
  • Energy module for modeling the different energy tarriffs, price, and renewable energy source types.
  • System module for modeling building energy consumption systems (heating, cooling, ventilation, etc.) and elements (boiler, chiller, radiaotor, etc.).
  • Occupant modulefor modeling occupants behaviour inside a building, their drivers, needs and actions to make underestandable the occupant-building interaction and different comfort states.
  • Service module for modeling services that are client of BEMServer.
Alignments are done between the different modules.
